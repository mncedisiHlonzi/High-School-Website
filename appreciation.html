HONORING OUR HEROES

At Shengeza High School, we stand on the shoulders of giants – individuals whose dedication, support, and generosity have helped shape our school into the vibrant community it is today. From educators to community members, alumni to local celebrities, we extend our deepest gratitude to all who have contributed their time, resources, and talents to enriching the lives of our students and fostering a culture of excellence. Your unwavering support has made a lasting impact on our school and the generations of learners who pass through our doors. We appreciate each and every one of you for your invaluable contributions to the Shengeza High School family.

Special Thanks to Mrs. NN ngubane

We are honored and grateful for your extraordinary dedication and pioneering spirit as the founder of Shengeza High School. Your visionary leadership and tireless efforts laid the foundation for our school's journey and left an indelible mark on our community.

In January 1995, amidst challenging circumstances, you embarked on the remarkable endeavor of establishing Shengeza High School. With only four classrooms and administrative tasks conducted under a tree, you demonstrated unparalleled determination and resilience. Through your unwavering commitment, you mobilized resources and rallied support, ensuring that teaching and learning could thrive despite the initial hardships.

Your leadership extended beyond the classroom as you spearheaded initiatives to expand the school's infrastructure and curriculum offerings. Your foresight in introducing streams in Commerce, General, and Science was groundbreaking, setting Shengeza High School apart as a trailblazer in the Umhlabuyalingana CMC.

Despite your subsequent promotion to the role of circuit manager at the uBombo circuit, your connection to Shengeza High School remained steadfast. Your legacy of excellence continues to inspire generations of learners and educators alike.

Mrs. Ngubane, your unwavering dedication, and profound impact on our school community are a testament to your extraordinary leadership and commitment to education. We are forever grateful for your visionary leadership, and your legacy will continue to shape the future of Shengeza High School for years to come.

“ I, Mrs. N. N. Ngubane, was the founder of Shengeza High School in January 1995. At its inception, the school had only four classrooms, all lacking a roof. Administrative tasks were carried out under a tree. I was assigned to this endeavor by Mr. AB Ntuli from the Ubonjeni Circuit Office. Initially, I was the sole teacher responsible for opening the school. Mr. CM Mlambo served as the chairperson, and together we mobilized funds to complete the roofing, enabling teaching and learning to commence.
Shengeza High School
Grade 8 and 9 students were enrolled in the inaugural year. Despite the challenging conditions, by the end of 1995, our staff had grown to include five teachers. However, there were still shortages of textbooks and desks. I quickly organized these essentials while overseeing the completion of the roofing project.
Shengeza High School
The following year marked the introduction of the first Grade 10 class, and by 1999, the school celebrated its first cohort of matriculants. Among them was KT Ngubane, who now serves as a teacher at Shengeza High School.
Shengeza High School
Initially, the school offered streams in Commerce, General, and Science, with Shengeza being the first high school to introduce Commerce in the Umhlabuyalingana CMC. I held the distinction of being the only female high school principal in the area at the time.
Shengeza High School
By the end of 2000, the infrastructure had expanded to include seven additional classrooms. In 2001, I was promoted to the role of circuit manager at the uBombo circuit. Despite this career advancement, Shengeza High School remained a source of immense pride for me, as I reflect on its remarkable achievements. ”
Shengeza High School
~ Mrs. NN ngubane ~
